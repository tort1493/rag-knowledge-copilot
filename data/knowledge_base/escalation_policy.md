# Escalation Policy

Support cases move to engineering escalation when the issue blocks production use, affects multiple users, or requires a code-level fix.

Severity levels are defined as follows. Sev 1 means the production service is unavailable for multiple customers. Sev 2 means a major workflow is degraded with no acceptable workaround. Sev 3 means a non-critical defect or single-user issue.

Sev 1 incidents require the incident commander to page the on-call engineering lead immediately and notify the customer success manager within 30 minutes. Sev 2 issues are triaged by engineering within one business day. Sev 3 issues remain in the standard backlog.

Only the incident commander can declare a Sev 1 incident. Support managers may recommend escalation, but they cannot declare a Sev 1 on their own.
